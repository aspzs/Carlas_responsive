"Tip not to tap."

Photoproject and photobook in form of a diary, that visualizes the very beginning of a first Corona lockdown.

Group exhibition for the “Festival fotografischer Bilder”, October 2020, Regensburg, Germany.
In the arrangement, the copies first try to analyze the happening and the situation in a theoretical way based on newspaper articles, notes, research and then the pictures just represent my daily life in that period with a self-portrait, a portrait of my flatmate and a more abstract one, that could refer to the virus and the idea of the unknown or inexperience while being in this situation.

Fragmente einer Spracher der Liebe 2.0.

Photoproject and self-made book re-conceptionalizing Roland Barthes „A lover‘s discourse“ with personal scetches, thoughts, drawings.
I have taken apart all the pages and put the book back together in a way that makes sense in my personal experience. The pages are sewn together and form their very own discourse.

December 2021, Munich, Germany.

reback reflash refresh renew restart.

Photoproject and photobook, that visualizes the Corona period in my childhood home in Munich at the end of the Corona crisis, starting from my usual point of view and reflecting the interpersonal relationships in our family from a distant view. In the arrangement, the pictures tell the story of the routine coming and going and the individual chapters are intended to remind us of the continuum in an everyday Corona life.

The book was launched at Offgrid-fotofestival in September 2022 in Vienna, Austria

Pictures and frontpage-illustration: Carla Schwering.
Graphic design: Lis Füreder.

Exuvie - humoralphatologischer Zyklus, Teil I : Melancholie.

Photoproject about the topic of the humoral theory that grew with the Humorism and began to fall out of favor in the 1850s with the advent of germ theory, which was able to show that many diseases previously thought to be humoral were in fact caused by microbes.

This project emphasizes on the melancholic temperament with the idea that the black bile that overdominates in the body causes an excessive amount of melancholic mood. I made photogramms out of textiles composed of silk and wax, to symbolize the fragility of our skin in it‘s different layers, which is considered to be characteristic of the external development of our body. I experimented with wax and photo emulsion to make photograms that also go through a development process in the darkroom and still show the fragility of the skin in it‘s evolution.

Work in progress since 2022, collaboration with photography student Timna Lugstein from Angewandte Wien, Austria.

Selected work shown at Folkwang Universität der Künste, January 2023, Essen, Germany.
